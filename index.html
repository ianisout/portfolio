About

I got started in tech when busting my aunt's computer by deleting "useless" windows files to open up storage to install games. I needed to understand why and how that happened. I messed her computer up a few times before starting to get the hang of it. A little later, I got a Technician degree — and I've been paying my dues by fixing computers for the entire family since then.

A couple of years ago, I scored a scholarship for a coding Bootcamp, diving head first on Full Stack Web Development, Node.js and React. I worked on a few projects as a web dev, until I had a taste of Unity and game building. Today, I work in a Technical Support position for Braintree/Paypal but spend most of my free time learning game development, C#, and watching GDC's videos to a somewhat dubitable degree.

I have a rather holistic background relevant to game design, which raises a feeling of "jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none" at times, but I believe it to be one of my biggest strengths. It arms me with a more comprehensive idea of how things work together in a game creation environment and how the ambiance of a gameplay can be affected as a whole. Plus, I can alter and fix many things independently before needing to go after paid licensed assets, which is nice!

I have studied music and some music production and also worked for a while with marketing/social media, honing my skills with visual tools such as Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, as well as video recording and editing with OBS and Adobe Premiere. I also hold an A.A. in Writing and Literature, as well as am pursuing a B.S. in Computer Engineering at this time. A little wild maybe but my heart is in the right place, I promise :)
